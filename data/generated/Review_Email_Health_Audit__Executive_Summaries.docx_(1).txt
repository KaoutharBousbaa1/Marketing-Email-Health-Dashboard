  Executive  Summaries  
1)  Did  engagement  decline  suddenly,  or  in  stages?  
It  declined  in  stages:  click  behavior  weakened  first,  then  opens  dropped  later.  
●  We  analyzed  6,854  canceled  subscribers.  ●  Overall  median  subscriber  lifespan  was  81  days,  but  recent  30-day  churn  median  was  194  days.  ●  CTOR  fell  first  from  12.6%  to  7.9%  during  Jun–Oct  2025.  ●  Open  rate  fell  later  from  40.3%  to  35.9%  during  Nov  2025–Feb  2026.  
This  matters  because  it  shows  the  issue  started  inside  the  email  (people  opened  but  clicked  less),  then  
moved
 
to
 
inbox
 
behavior
 
(people
 
opened
 
less).
 
So
 
this
 
is
 
not
 
only
 
a
 
subject-line
 
problem;
 
message
 
relevance
 
and
 
CTA
 
quality
 
are
 
central
 
to
 
the
 
decline.
 
 
2)  Are  older  subscribers  disengaging  more  than  newer  subscribers?  
Older  subscribers  are  clearly  less  engaged  than  newer  subscribers.  
●  Oldest  signup  segment:  Open  rate  25.6%,  CTOR  18.9%.  ●  Newest  signup  segment:  Open  rate  40.7%,  CTOR  33.8%.  
The  decline  is  not  evenly  spread  across  the  list.  A  lot  of  weakness  is  concentrated  in  older  segments,  
which
 
points
 
to
 
audience-message
 
mismatch
 
over
 
time.
 
The
 
practical

is
 
to
 
stop

as

and
 
use

tracks.
 
 
3)  Did  value  emails  and  sales  emails  decline  in  the  same  way?  
Both  declined,  but  sales  emails  weakened  more  sharply  overall.  
●  Value  (Feb–May  2025  vs  Nov  2025–Feb  2026):  OR  42.1%  to  36.0%;  CTOR  13.9%  to  7.9%.  ●  Sales  (same  periods):  OR  56.5%  to  33.0%;  CTOR  19.2%  to  7.7%.  
Value  content  still  gets  opened  at  a  reasonable  level,  but  fewer  readers  take  action.  Sales  content  took  a  
stronger
 
hit
 
on
 
both
 
opens
 
and
 
clicks,
 
which
 
means
 
both
 
attention
 
and
 
persuasion
 
fell.
 
In
 
short:
 
value
 
has
 
a
 
click
 
quality
 
problem;
 
sales
 
has
 
both
 
a
 
reach
 
and
 
click
 
problem.

6)  Do  buyers  of  the  bootcamps  behave  differently  from  non-buyers?  
Buyers  are  much  more  engaged  on  both  opens  and  clicks.  
●  Stable-month  comparison  (Feb  2025–Feb  2026):  buyers  OR  63.3%  vs  non-buyers  41.7%.  ●  Buyers  CTOR  53.8%  vs  non-buyers  28.4%.  
Purchase  history  is  one  of  the  strongest  engagement  signals  in  the  whole  audit.  These  subscribers  are  
not
 
just

once;
 
they
 
continue
 
to
 
behave
 
like

8)  How  long  does  it  usually  take  from  signup  to  first  bootcamp  purchase?  
Typical  conversion  is  around  two  months,  with  a  meaningful  long  tail.  
●  Median  time  to  first  purchase:  58  days.  ●  Average  time:  79.8  days.  ●  27.3%  convert  within  30  days.  ●  69.5%  convert  within  90  days.  ●  12.7%  convert  after  180  days.  
Most  conversions  happen  in  the  first  three  months,  but  later  conversions  are  still  important.  That  
supports
 
a
 
mixed
 
cadence
 
strategy:
 
early
 
conversion
 
pushes
 
plus
 
steady
 
medium-term

9)  Which  subscriber  groups  are  currently  the  strongest?  
Three  groups  stand  out:  buyers,  lead-magnet  path  subscribers,  and  newer  subscribers.  
●  Bootcamp  buyers  show  the  strongest  ongoing  OR  and  CTOR  vs  non-buyers.  ●  Lead-magnet  path  subscribers  show  higher  post-survey  OR  and  sales  CTOR  vs  existing  subscribers.  ●  Newer  subscribers  outperform  oldest  signup  segments.   The  list  is  uneven,  not  uniformly  weak.  Strong  performance  still  exists,  but  in  specific  pockets.  Growth  
will
 
come
 
from
 
amplifying
 
those
 
pockets
 
rather
 
than
 
averaging
 
everyone

Final  Takeaway  
Email  engagement  is  declining  overall,  but  the  decline  is  concentrated  in  older  subscribers,  not  the  whole  
list.
 
Newer
 
subscribers
 
and
 
people
 
who
 
already
 
bought
 
are
 
still
 
much
 
more
 
responsive,
 
so
 
the
 
list
 
still
 
has
 
strong
 
potential.
 
The  lead  magnet  is  useful,  mainly  as  a  qualification  channel:  people  coming  through  that  path  are  much  
more
 
likely
 
to
 
open
 
sales
 
emails,
 
even
 
if
 
lift
 
is
 
not
 
uniform
 
across
 
every
 
metric.
 
The  core  problem  now  is  both  reach  and  action:  fewer  people  are  opening  over  time  in  older  segments,  
and
 
click
 
action
 
is
 
weaker
 
in
 
key
 
periods.
 
To  stop  the  decline,  focus  on  segmentation  by  subscriber  age  and  intent,  tighten  message-to-CTA  flow  in  
both
 
value
 
and
 
sales
 
emails,
 
and
 
run
 
a
 
dedicated
 
re-engagement
 
plan
 
for
 
older
 
inactive
 
subscribers.
 
Workshops  remain  an  important  path  into  bootcamp,  but  performance  varies  by  cohort,  so  execution  
quality
 
(offer,
 
timing,
 
handoff)
 
matters
 
more
 
than
 
volume.
